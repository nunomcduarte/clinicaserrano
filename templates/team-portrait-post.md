# Template — Team Portrait Post

A single-image post introducing a member of the clinical team. The most "human" surface in the system: it carries the people behind the practice.

## When to use

- Introducing a new team member.
- Re-surfacing an existing team member tied to a specialty being highlighted (e.g. promoting maternal care → Patrícia Machado).
- Anniversary, milestone, or achievement of a team member.

## Image

- **Format:** vertical portrait, native to Instagram 4:5.
- **Style:** see `brand/05-photography.md`. Warm, available light, candid posture, shallow DoF.
- **Subject styling:** wearing the role-appropriate brand application (lab coat for clinicians, branded t-shirt for allied health). The wordmark is visible but never the focus.
- **Background:** real environment — a clinic room, a treatment space, a lit interior. Never a photographic backdrop or grey seamless.
- **Single subject** in frame. No group portraits in this template.

## Caption

Three beats, in **Register A** (formal, third person). Always third person — even if the subject is the founder, the brand speaks *about* them, not *as* them.

```
[Opening: a balanced two-part pairing — "X by Y, Z by W"]

[Credentials: registry number + qualifications + advanced training + named mentors/institutions if relevant]

[Philosophy: how they practice, what they value]
```

### Opening line rules

- **Always a balanced pair** — two short noun phrases linked by a comma.
- Format: `[Role] por [reason], [other role] por [reason].`
- Format alt: `[Profession] de [domain], [profession] de [domain].`
- 5–8 words total. No more.
- Sets the **human** before the **credentials**.

Examples observed and adjacent:
- *Médico por vocação, desportista por paixão.* (Dr. Serrano)
- *Profissional de rigor, presença de coração.* (adjacent style, fictional)
- *Enfermeira por formação, conselheira por entrega.* (adjacent style, fictional — note: Patrícia's actual post used a different opener; this is a stylistic example)

For Patrícia Machado specifically, the live caption opens with the credentials directly rather than a balanced pair — confirm whether the opening pair is **always required** or **only used for senior/founding figures**.

### Credentials paragraph rules

- Open with the person's name. Use `Dr.` / `Dra.` / first name appropriately.
- Cite registry/professional number in parentheses immediately after the name. This is non-negotiable for regulated professions in PT.
- List qualifications in order: degree → specialty → post-grad → continuing education / mentors.
- Name relevant mentors or institutions only if the reader will recognize them as authority signals.
- One paragraph. 2–4 sentences.

### Philosophy paragraph rules

- Describes how the person practices, not what they do.
- Anchors to brand vocabulary: `rigor`, `empatia`, `presença`, `cuidado`, `escuta`, `respeito ao ritmo`.
- Avoids superlatives.
- One paragraph. 1–3 sentences.

## What this template is *not*

- ❌ Not a place for the team member's contact info or booking link. Keep that in bio/links.
- ❌ Not a service description. The post is about the **person**, not what they treat.
- ❌ Not first-person ("Sou a Patrícia, e..."). The brand always speaks third person about its team.

## Provisional

- Whether the opening balanced pair is mandatory or only used for senior figures.
- Whether team-member posts ever come as a multi-slide carousel (cover slide + portrait + bio).
- Whether the team member's name is ever overlaid graphically on the image, or only ever in the caption.
