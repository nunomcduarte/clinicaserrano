# Template — Space & Object Photography Post

Single-image posts that show the clinic itself: rooms, equipment, details. These are atmospheric posts — they sell the *experience* of being in the clinic without ever selling.

## When to use

- Introducing a new room or service space.
- Showing equipment as still life (especially branded objects: towels, weights, tools).
- Sustaining feed rhythm between heavier service-led carousels.

## Subject categories observed

| Subject | Treatment | Captures |
|---|---|---|
| Treatment room / interior | Wide-ish frame, warm light, branded objects in frame | The atmosphere of the clinic |
| Gym / training room | Wide frame, deeper DoF, equipment visible end-to-end | The "we have a real space" message |
| Equipment close-up | Tight crop, shallow DoF, single tool group | The materiality and quality of tools |

## Image

- **Format:** vertical portrait, 4:5.
- **Light:** see `brand/05-photography.md`. Warm, soft, **the light itself is often the subject** — shelf lighting, window light through curtains.
- **Composition:** symmetric or near-symmetric framing for hero interiors; offset framing for object close-ups. Plenty of negative space.
- **Branded objects in-frame** are encouraged but never forced. A folded `SERRANOCLÍNICA` towel in the background reinforces the brand without making the photo feel like an ad.
- **No people** in this template (people get the team-portrait template).

## Caption

In **Register A** (formal, sensory, two-line poetic). The caption matches the photograph's quietness — it does not narrate what the viewer can already see.

```
[1 line — sensory or value statement about the space]
[1 line — what that space enables / the human payoff]
```

Examples observed:

> *Ambientes que respiram leveza, profissionais que inspiram confiança.*
> *É neste equilíbrio que o bem-estar acontece.*

Adjacent shape (fictional, for reference):
> *Cada detalhe foi pensado para ser recebido em silêncio.*
> *É na calma que o corpo recupera.*

### What captions don't do

- ❌ They don't describe the photo. ("Aqui pode ver-se a nossa nova sala...")
- ❌ They don't push a CTA. (Use the CTA template for that.)
- ❌ They don't list features. ("Iluminação LED, equipamento Nike, soalho de borracha...")

The **rule of restraint**: if the caption could appear under any *good* clinic interior photo, you've written it too generic. If it could only appear under *this specific photo*, you've over-described it. Aim for the middle: emotionally specific to this space, lyrically open.

## Object close-ups variant

For tight equipment shots (the dumbbell-detail post observed), the caption rules above still apply, but there's a sub-pattern:

- The caption may pivot to **service CTA** (Register B), in which case treat it as a CTA post (`templates/service-cta-post.md` — TBD).
- Or it may stay in Register A, treating the equipment as material poetry. Both are valid.
