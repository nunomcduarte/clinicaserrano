# Visual Foundations

One-page summary of the Clínica Serrano visual identity. Detailed specs live in the sibling files in this folder.

## Mood

Warm, quiet, premium-clinical. Reads more like a wellness/editorial brand than a hospital. Two surface modes coexist:

**Typographic surfaces** (carousel slides, copy-led posts) — two variants:

- **Greige variant** (default, premium/atmospheric posts): warm-greige paper background, off-white cream type, generous negative space.
- **Cream variant** (announcements, urgency, alerts): cream/off-white background, dark charcoal type, same restraint and spacing. Used when the message is time-sensitive or directional ("flu season is coming, here's how we help"). The brand's way of signalling *attention* without resorting to bright colors.

Both variants share the same typography and the same one-idea-per-surface discipline. They simply **invert the contrast** to flag a different intent in the feed.

**Photographic surfaces** (team portraits, interiors, equipment, candids):
- Warm, low-key, available-light photography. No flash, no clinical white-light look.
- Shallow depth of field; subjects shown in candid moments (a doctor mid-listen, a smile mid-conversation).
- Interiors emphasize wood, soft fabric, light strips, plants — the paper-greige spirit translated into materials.
- Brand presence shows up as embroidered/printed wordmark on uniforms and accessories.

**Hybrid surfaces** (deep-dive carousel covers): a photograph fills the canvas; the headline is set on top. Permitted only on **action / atmospheric / motion-blur** photography — not on portraits or candid clinical scenes (which stand alone, never with text overlay).

The variants are designed to **alternate in the feed**. Type goes on paper. Portraits stand alone. Action shots may carry a headline.

## The five building blocks

1. **Paper-texture background** — warm greige (default) or cream (announcement variant). Full-bleed on typographic surfaces.
2. **The wordmark** — `SERRANO` in a high-contrast Didone serif paired with `CLÍNICA` in a light all-caps sans. Position is contextual (top-right, top-center, bottom-left, bottom-center) — see `03-logo.md`.
3. **A geometric display sans** — large for headlines, smaller for body, smallest for the `CLÍNICA` lockup. Bold weight is reserved for inline emphasis on key terms.
4. **Warm photography** — low-key, available-light, candid. Subjects matter more than composition tricks.
5. **A pill-shaped arrow CTA** — outlined, used to signal "swipe" on multi-slide carousels. Variants exist for "tap-to-act" CTAs (e.g. the wider `Consulta Urgente | Consulta ao Domicílio` pill on the gripe cover slide).

## What this brand is *not*

A reference list of things the system avoids — useful when commissioning new assets:

- ❌ No medical iconography on typographic surfaces (no crosses, no stethoscopes, no DNA helices).
- ❌ No stock photography. Real team, real spaces, real equipment.
- ❌ No bright primary accents. No blue/green "trust palette."
- ❌ No drop shadows, gradients, or 3D.
- ❌ No multiple fonts beyond the defined family. Italic / oblique not in use.
- ❌ No dense layouts. One idea per surface.
- ❌ No text overlay on portraits or candid clinical photography. (Headline-on-action-photo is fine for hero slides; headline-on-doctor-portrait is not.)
- ❌ No flash photography or hard, cool clinical lighting.
